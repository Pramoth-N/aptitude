
## 1. ₹500 using ₹100 and ₹20 notes

### Question
In how many ways can X give ₹500 to Y using only ₹100 and ₹20 notes, with at most 16 ₹20 notes and both denominations used?

### Answer
**3 ways**

### Derivation

Let:

- ₹100 notes = x
- ₹20 notes = y

Equation:

\[
100x + 20y = 500
\]

\[
5x + y = 25
\]

Conditions:

- y ≤ 16
- x ≥ 1
- y ≥ 1

Possible values:

| ₹100 Notes | ₹20 Notes |
|-----------|-----------|
| 2 | 15 |
| 3 | 10 |
| 4 | 5 |

Total ways:

\[
\boxed{3}
\]

## 6. Compound Interest Loan

### Question
Monthly installment = ₹9075 for 2 years at 10% compound interest. Find principal.

### Answer

**₹180000**

### Derivation

Total repayment:

\[
9075\times24=217800
\]

Formula:

\[
A=P(1+r)^n
\]

\[
217800=P(1.1)^2
\]

\[
P=180000
\]

## 7. Average Marks Increase

### Question
20% students averaging 48 removed; average increases by 10%. Find original average.

### Answer

**80**

### Derivation

Assume 100 students.

Original marks:

\[
100x
\]

Removed:

\[
20\times48=960
\]

Remaining:

\[
80(1.1x)
\]

Equation:

\[
100x-960=88x
\]

\[
x=80
\]
