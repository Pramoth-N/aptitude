## 2. What is the age of Sumit?

Statements:

I. Sumit is five years younger than Amit, who is double the age of Prateek.

II. Prateek is 10 years older than Ravi, whose age is 22 years.

---

## Options

- Both statements I and II are not sufficient ❌  
- Statement II alone is sufficient ❌  
- Statement I alone is sufficient ❌  
- ✅ **Both statements I and II are necessary**

---

## Answer

**Both statements I and II are necessary**

---

## Explanation

Using **Statement I only**:

\[
\text{Sumit}=2(\text{Prateek})-5
\]

Prateek's age is unknown.

Hence:

**Statement I alone → Not sufficient**

---

Using **Statement II only**:

\[
\text{Prateek}=22+10=32
\]

No relation to Sumit.

Hence:

**Statement II alone → Not sufficient**

---

Using **both statements together**:

From Statement II:

\[
\text{Prateek}=32
\]

From Statement I:

\[
\text{Amit}=2\times32=64
\]

\[
\text{Sumit}=64-5=59
\]

Age of Sumit is determined uniquely.

Therefore:

✅ **Both statements I and II are necessary**

## 3.

Statements:

1. All vegetables are fruits  
2. No carrot is fruit  
3. Some roots are carrot  
4. All roots are eatables  

Conclusions:

I. Some fruits are eatables  
II. No root is vegetable  
III. Some eatables are carrots  
IV. Some roots are vegetables  

---

## Options

- Only I and either II or IV follow ❌  
- ✅ **Only III and either II or IV follow**  
- Only conclusion II, III and IV follow ❌  
- Only conclusion III and IV follow ❌  

---

## Answer

**Only III and either II or IV follow**

---

## Explanation

Convert statements logically:

- Vegetables ⊆ Fruits  
- Carrots ∩ Fruits = ∅  
- Some Roots → Carrots  
- Roots ⊆ Eatables  

From:

Some Roots are Carrots  
All Roots are Eatables

Therefore:

Some Carrots are Eatables

Hence:

✅ Conclusion III follows

\[
\text{Some eatables are carrots}
\]

---

Check Conclusion II:

No root is vegetable

Roots overlap with carrots, and carrots cannot be fruits.

Since:

Vegetables ⊆ Fruits

Carrots cannot be vegetables.

Some roots are carrots → those roots cannot be vegetables.

This suggests **some roots are not vegetables**, but not **all roots**.

So Conclusion II is **not definitely true**.

---

Check Conclusion IV:

Some roots are vegetables

No evidence supports this.

Not definite.

---

Between II and IV:

- Either roots overlap vegetables
- Or they don't

Both cannot be true together.

Thus:

✅ **Either II or IV follows**

---

Check Conclusion I:

Some fruits are eatables

No direct relation between fruits and eatables.

Cannot conclude.

❌ Conclusion I does not follow

---

Final:

✅ **Only III and either II or IV follow**

## 4. Question

The figure marked **X** is folded into a box. Identify which boxed figures can be formed.

---
![alt text](image-1.png)

### Options

- B and D only ❌  
- A and D only ❌  
- A and B only ❌  
- ✅ **B and C only**

---

### Answer

**B and C only**

---

### Explanation

From the net:

Opposite faces become:

- `*` is opposite `X`
- `□` (square) is opposite `•` (dot)
- The two blank faces are opposite each other

Check options:

- **A:** `□` and `•` appear adjacent → impossible (they are opposite) ❌  
- **B:** `*` and `•` are adjacent → possible ✅  
- **C:** `X` and `□` are adjacent → possible ✅  
- **D:** `X` and `*` are adjacent → impossible (they are opposite) ❌  

Therefore, only:

✅ **B and C can be formed**
